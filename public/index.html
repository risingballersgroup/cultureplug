Risk Guardrails
How much of your capital you risk on a single trade.
Example: With £50 capital and 2% risk, you risk £1 per trade. If your stop is 3% below entry, the system buys £33 worth of stock — so a full stop-out loses exactly £1.
The maximum you're willing to lose in a single day before stopping all trading.
Example: Set to £5 (10% of £50 capital). If your trades hit stops totalling £5 in one day, the AI will refuse to suggest new setups until tomorrow. Prevents revenge trading spirals.
Maximum number of trades open at the same time.
Example: Set to 3. With £50 at 2% risk each, you have 3 × £1 = £3 at risk simultaneously — less than 6% of capital exposed at any one time. More positions = less focus and more complexity.
Minimum reward-to-risk ratio. Only take trades where the potential gain is at least this many times the potential loss.
Example: R:R of 2 means for every £1 you risk, you target £2 profit. At a 40% win rate with 1:2 R:R you still make money long-term. Never take trades below this ratio — it's how you stay profitable even when you're wrong half the time.
